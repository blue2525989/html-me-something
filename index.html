Motorcycles

Weather permitting I enjoy riding my motorcycle. I own a 2001 Suzuki Katana 750. It looks like one of the fast sporty bikes, and it is, but I drive more casually. I enjoy riding on rural country back roads by my house where there is rarely another car. The most dangerous thing about riding motorcycles are other drivers on the road. My girl friend and I enjoy going for a short cruise on the weekends.


Farming

One of my favorite summer activities is farming. My family and I have about a 400 square foot organic vegetable garden. Each year we plant a lot of peas, green beans, carrots, lettuces, kale, and various other veggies. It is great for my six year old daughter. She enjoys going out and picking fresh peas and green beans straight from the plant to her mouth. I never have to make her eat her veggies, if anything I have to tell her to slow down and let the plants grow a little. Also it teaches her the importance of being conscious of nature.

Also we have three goats. Totes ma goats, which is at the bottom of the page, is a Nigerian Dwarf goat. Buddy which is at the top of the page is half Nigerian dwarf and half fainting goat. Last we have a goat named Rosemary whom is a fainting goat. She is the mother of buddy. Also she is quite comical when she faints and either stops in her tracks and locks up, or tips over and makes weird noises for about ten seconds then gets back to playing.


Hiking

When it is nice out my family and I enjoy hiking. The picture above is of the highest waterfall in Missouri. It is located on 'Taum Sauk mountain' about two hours south of St. Louis. Not only is it the tallest waterfall in the state, the mountain itself is the highest peak in the state. Time permitting you can visit the tallest waterfall and highest peak in Missouri in one afternoon. Just be prepared for the hike to the waterfall, the trail is rough at best.
